The Brief

They had a presence. It just didn't look like the food tasted.

A lot of restaurants are in this exact spot — they're active on social, they're showing up, but the content is letting them down. Phone shots in bad light, inconsistent style, nothing that makes someone stop scrolling and think "I need to go there."

Mother Chorizo's had the personality and the product. They just needed someone who knew how to capture it — and who understood how to make content that actually performs on social, not just content that exists.
